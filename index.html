Digital Supply Network Transformation Strategy for Lion New Zealand

Project Challenge

The project addressed the need for Lion New Zealand to fully leverage the digital tools available within its supply network. The main objectives of the project were to identify gaps in the use of digital tools within Lion New Zealand’s supply network and to propose a strategic transformation approach that could improve supply network operations and digital maturity.

My Involvement

This project was completed as an individual assignment. I conducted the organisational analysis, applied strategic frameworks, and developed recommendations for improving the company’s digital supply network capabilities.

Approach

The analysis applied several strategic frameworks to evaluate the organisation’s current situation and identify improvement opportunities. These included a SWOT analysis and the use of corporate-level and business-level strategy matrices to examine the company’s competitive position and strategic direction.

Outcome

The final outcome of the project was the development of a strategic roadmap designed to improve digital supply network maturity and system integration within Lion New Zealand.

04 — Reflective Learning

Eight Moments of Reflection

01 / 08
Process Analysis
Workflow Improvement

During my role at DearDoc, I identified an operational issue during the client onboarding process. The product team followed a standardised workflow to set up new client products t. were product edits were expected only after the initial setup stage. However, client edit requests were added in the case notes during the setup phase. Because the product team followed the workflow strictly and did not routinely check these notes during setup, some client requests were delayed or overlooked.

I coordinated with the onboarding team manager to review the workflow and identify a solution. We agreed that any edits requested before the editing stage would be recorded in a separate case linked to the initial setup case. This ensured that the product team could clearly identify pending edits once the setup phase was completed.

02 / 08
Coordination
Key coordinator

During my role as a Food Technologist and Laboratory Assistant at Destilería del Valle de Tequila, I coordinated a client project involving the development of a spiced infused tequila product. I acted as the communication link between the client, the tequila master, and the production team. My responsibilities included translation, coordination of experimental production runs, and managing the client’s raw materials.

The client initially wanted to use a small-scale infusion method involving spice-filled teabags. After discussing the process with the production team, we adapted the method by adding spices directly into the liquid and later applying filtration techniques to clarify the product. The first production batch was successfully completed and bottled in the following weeks.

03 / 08
Documentation Accuracy
Attention to Detail

At the tequila distillery I was responsible for processing laboratory samples to verify alcohol proof levels for margarita product batches. These measurements required precision because the alcohol content had to match the product label and client specifications. Accurate documentation determined whether a batch could be approved and released for distribution.

At DearDoc I also contributed to the creation and maintenance of Standard Operating Procedures used by the team. These documents supported training and created a knowledge base that improved onboarding efficiency and allowed the team to quickly reference previous solutions when addressing similar client requests.

04 / 08
Adaptability
Navigating Setbacks & Pressure

Working on the grill at Cheese on Toast requires constant adaptation to changing service conditions. During busy periods, multiple orders arrive simultaneously and preparation processes must be adjusted accordingly. For example, when several toastie orders arrive at the same time, retrieving ingredients in batches rather than individually reduces unnecessary movement and improves efficiency.

Managing these situations requires prioritising tasks, starting new preparation processes while completing existing ones, and maintaining coordination with other team members to ensure orders are delivered in the correct sequence.
